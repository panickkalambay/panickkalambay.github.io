You can browse my publications, ongoing projects, courses I teach, and academic activities.

I am currently an Assistant Professor of Civil Engineering (Transportation) at Texas Southern University (TSU), where I joined in Fall 2025. My main research interests are traffic safety and transportation planning.

Before joining TSU, I was a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Washington Tacoma from 2023 to 2025. I completed my Ph.D. in Civil Engineering (Transportation) at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte in 2023. I received my master's degree in Civil Engineering (Transportation) from the University of Osaka and my bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering from the University of Kinshasa.

I have experience leveraging GIS capabilities and applying advanced statistical methods, as well as machine and deep learning techniques, including computer vision algorithms, to address transportation-related research problems. My approach is multidisciplinary and interdisciplinary, drawing on data science, transportation engineering, and urban planning.

I look for motivated master’s students interested in traffic safety and transportation planning. Interested candidates are encouraged to contact me at panick.kalambay@tsu.edu with their resume and a brief statement of research interests.

Research Interests
  • Traffic Safety: Crash frequency modeling,injury severity analysis, road safety policy evaluation, hotspot identification
  • Transportation Planning: Urban mobility, active transportation, connected and automated vehicles
  • GIS & Spatial Analysis: Spatial regression models, geographically weighted regression, network analysis, accessibility mapping
  • Machine & Deep Learning: Computer vision for traffic monitoring, object detection, convolutional neural networks, predictive modeling
  • Statistical Methods: Bayesian inference, count data models, survival analysis, mixed-effects models applied to transportation data
